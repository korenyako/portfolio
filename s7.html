S7 website itinerary review page

S7 is one of the Russia's largest airlines. The company operates to 150 destinations within Russia and internationally. I conducted a series of interviews with potential customers and frequent travellers to find possible bottlenecks and errors to improve the itinerary review page.

My Role

S7 Airlines asked me to propose improvement ideas for the itinerary review page. This page allows S7 customers to review the selected flight itinerary and add ancillary products.

What I did

  • Interviews
  • User Flows
  • Mockups

I found that unfolded ancillary offers have unnecessary options and information that divert visitors away from the goals. The services which are already boooked, get lost in the total list. Red plates for Departure/Arrival time in the ticket look as warning or urgency and conflict with "Proceed with the payment" button. There's no difference in style between main and ancillary products. The users has feelings that ancillary products are obligatory for buying and not optional. Same products on itinerary page and in the shopping cart have different names. There's no logic in design of similar products: different buttons for the same actions, different font sizes, etc. Some of the elements are not properly aligned.

As a result I came up with several ideas that can be combined with the phrase "Sort Things Out!" There's no timer showing how much time left for the airplane ticket's reservation.

Remove distraction. Let visitors focus on a single action and not be distracted from it. Split itinerary page into a set of single screens. Now user will be more focused on his goals. Also you can add more ancillaries in the future.
